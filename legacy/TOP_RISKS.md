# Top 10 Risks

| Rank | Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Customers do not pay for privacy or verification | High | High | Interview target users, run paid pilots, measure willingness to pay |
| 2 | Inference does not beat mining after real costs | Medium | High | Measure GPU-specific mining/inference crossover with real workloads |
| 3 | Trusted controller prevents credible decentralization claims | High | Medium | Be explicit about current state; validate trusted-operator use cases first |
| 4 | Verification is too weak or too expensive | Medium | High | Benchmark verification overhead and adversarial accuracy |
| 5 | Sybil and collusion attacks break committees/reputation | High | High | Avoid public-network claims until identity and verifier selection are solved |
| 6 | Qi circulates poorly and is hoarded | Medium | High | Track velocity, hoarding, and reinvestment in closed pilots |
| 7 | Local-first setup is too hard for operators | Medium | Medium | Test onboarding with self-hosters and local businesses |
| 8 | Competing APIs win on convenience, latency, and model quality | High | Medium | Focus on privacy/local-control niches instead of general API competition |
| 9 | Legal/privacy obligations exceed prototype controls | Medium | High | Map data roles, retention, regional constraints, and compliance gaps |
| 10 | Repo complexity grows faster than validation | Medium | Medium | Freeze subsystem expansion; prioritize measurement and assumption tests |

## Summary

The largest risks are not implementation bugs. They are market demand, real economics, trust, and verification validity.
